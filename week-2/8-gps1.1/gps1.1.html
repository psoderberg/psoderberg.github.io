What is pair programming?

Pair programming is collaborating on a project between two developers in real time. It typically involves a driver and a navigator to divide the responsibilities.

Why is pair programming important to DBC?

Not only does pair programming assist with better learning, it also prepares us for real world programming.

Compare Driver and Navigator.

The driver is responsible for performing all the actions regarding the project, while the navigator is responsible for directing and overseeing the driver's actions.

What was it like to pair for the first time? Did you enjoy it? What worked? What didn't work?

We enjoyed this pairing session. Dividing the roles into driving and navigating worked well. We switched roles half-way and continued to be successful. Stypi didn't work, but we overcame that glitch by using screenshare. At first, our header wouldn't stay at the top. After a little trial and error, we found the key to success by using CSS instead of HTML.
